Molly Malone's Exotic Animals

The Karala is a rare, fun-loving animal found on the McGey Islands. The karala is a friendly animal, but It can get fiesty when its neighbouring animal, the agnes, is offended. Karalas are natural preditors to the Meergats and the Savgulls. Karalas will sleep anywhere: on the floor, in a tree, anywhere! The Karalas only take dust baths once every three weeks and, overall, are pretty independent. DO NOT BUY THE KARALA IF YOU HAVE A CAT. Karalas love cats and will steal them and run away.

The Sealisa is an exotic bread of seal that inhabits the beaches of Mangere Bridge. It is an energetic seal that enjoys being active, you will spot the Sealisa playing in the water with its best buddy the Savgull. Sealisa and Savgull are together most of the time and need to be bought together. The Savgull can be moody but can also be really fun, it's favourite thing to do is lounge about in the sun sqwaking at people that walk by. Savgull and Sealisa need a spacious area that has water and land with plenty of food and sun. They both are very friendly animals that can get along with most people.

Ana the Stallion is a fun, funny, happy stallion that enjoys running around in the fields and making freinds. Ana the Stallion needs lots of water and food to stay happy and healthy. Having a lot of outside space will benefit Ana and keep her active. Ana the stallion, originally came from Stallion land is where there are loads of different kinds of stallions. Having other animals around will make Ana very happy. Ana's favourite thing to do is run around in the fields eating grass and chilling outside in the sun.

Kaity The Goat is a active, determined energetic baby goat. Kaity gets along well in groups of big people and love challanging her mind with games. Kaity's also gets along well with other animals and thrives in big feilds that she can run around in. Katies favourite food to eat is KFC, she loves the gravy and mashed potatoes. Kaity is a pygmy goat which is one of the nicest goat breeds as she will not eat your clothes. Kaity the Goat is from Lithuania, she came to New Zealand when she snuck onto a plane that was going to South Sudan (her third favourite country) but took control of it and instead landed it safely outside the 10M Mall. Ever since then Kaity has been in my care. She deserves to have a happy, loving home.

The Meergats are distant cousins of Meerkats. The Meergats come from Iraqiania, their fur is thin but cozy, perfect for the climate in Iraqiania. The nights are freezing cold but the days are scorching hot. To not get burnt alive they make holes very deep in the ground so that they can keep cool while they sleep during the day,but at night them come out to scavenge careful to avoid the invasive preditors the Karalas. Meergats like to keep to themsleves and will almost never makes friends outside their pack. however, they are very friendly animals and with a bit of a psuh can make loads of friends. The Meergats would thrive in a busy house with lots of places to hide. Their favourite is anything Italian.
